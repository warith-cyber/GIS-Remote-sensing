02 / The challenge

Klang needs monitoring at the scale of lived exposure.

The report identifies a gap in sparse municipal air-quality monitoring: a few fixed stations cannot capture the sharp pollution changes that occur around roads, industrial areas and sensitive public facilities.

01

Hyper-local exposure

Traffic emissions, road dust and industrial activity can vary strongly within 200 to 500 m.

02

Vulnerable receptors

Schools and hospitals are priority anchors because children, patients and elderly groups face higher health risks.

03

Evidence-based sensing

BME680 IoT nodes and LoRaWAN telemetry can support a denser monitoring network when placement is GIS-driven.

Design question

Where should sensors be placed so they monitor both emission pressure and the people most exposed to it?

03 / Study area

Klang District: port, industry and vulnerable communities.

Klang District was selected because major maritime, logistics and manufacturing activities sit close to schools and healthcare facilities. The GIS layers are clipped to the Klang boundary and kept in GDM2000 / Selangor Grid for analysis.

04 / Methodology

Two-stage weighted overlay for sensor allocation.

The report separates pollution causes from vulnerable receptors, then combines them so high suitability appears only where emission pressure overlaps schools and hospitals.

  1. 01

    Buffer

    Apply 300 m, 600 m and 900 m multi-ring buffers to roads, industries, schools and hospitals.

  2. 02

    Score

    Use Field Calculator scoring to translate proximity into 1-5 risk and vulnerability classes.

  3. 03

    Rasterise

    Convert vector criteria into aligned rasters using the same CRS, extent, origin and cell size.

  4. 04

    Allocate

    Overlay causal and impact rasters, classify the result and place sensors on high-priority cells.

08 / Discussion

Central industrial corridors carry the strongest exposure signal.

01

Industrial-logistics hotspots

The report finds the strongest risk around Klang's central industrial and logistics corridors where highways meet manufacturing zones.

02

Receptors change priority

Suitability increases when causal pressure intersects schools and hospitals, because the model prioritizes vulnerable populations.

03

Sensor buffers create coverage

Each proposed IoT node receives a 500 m coverage buffer to support localized AQI monitoring and interpolation between nodes.

Planning principle

Use GIS to reduce manual placement bias, then validate candidate sites through field access, calibration and LoRaWAN coverage checks.

09 / Conclusion

From spatial evidence to an accountable monitoring network.

GIS-based MCDM provides a defensible way to prioritize scarce IoT sensors in Klang. By connecting emission infrastructure with schools and hospitals, the model directs monitoring toward locations where real-time information can protect public health.

Recommendation

Deploy the first network across high-suitability zones, validate the BME680 readings in the field, then improve the model with weather, wind direction and real traffic observations.
